Growing Threats of Cybercrime

Author: Soumya Agarwal

The cyber attack on Marks & Spencer last year reveals the growing threat of cybercrime amidst the rapidly expanding digital dimensions in India. Cybercrime is a serious challenge in India today because increasing digital dependence is leading to a rise in cases of fraud, data theft, online scams, and the misuse of personal and political information, putting pressure on security, privacy, and legal action.

Major Cyber security Threats Facing India

  • Rise in financial fraud
  • Cyber espionage agencies
  • Ransom demands for digital assets
  • Exploitation of youth and false job promises
  • Emerging threat of cryptojacking
  • Advanced Persistent Threats (APTs)
  • Exploitation of cloud and API vulnerabilities
  • AI-powered deepfakes and chatbot phishing

Cybercrime

Any type of criminal activity planned by an individual or an entire organization that targets computer information holders, infrastructure, and computer networks with the aim of damaging or destroying the targeted computer network or system.

Cybercrime Includes

Hacking, illegal financial transactions/money laundering, copyright infringement, harassment by sending offensive content over the internet, ransomware (i.e. cyber extortion), exploits, social engineering, logic bombs, spamming, virus attacks, spoofing, denial of service, cryptojacking, breach of access, voice phishing, man-in-the-middle attacks etc.

These crimes transcend borders and can affect national security, financial systems, and personal safety. Developing countries are particularly vulnerable due to weak cyber security infrastructure and a lack of awareness. Emerging technologies like AI, deepfakes, and generative tools have created new risks related to misinformation, privacy, and copyright infringement.

Conclusion

In today's digital world, threats like cybercrime cannot be guaranteed against by technology alone. Robust security requires a dual strategy that combines technical safeguards with user awareness and responsible behaviour. While security tools help detect and prevent threats, informed users play a crucial role in recognizing hidden dangers such as phishing, scams, and unsafe online practices. By integrating technical security with cyber security awareness, individuals and organizations can establish a much stronger defence against evolving cyber threats.
